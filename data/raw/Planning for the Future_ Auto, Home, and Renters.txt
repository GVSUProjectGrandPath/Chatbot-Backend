Auto, Home, and Renters
Introduction (30 sec)
When financing a car, home, or renting a property, understanding loan requirements and the right insurance coverage is crucial. Let’s break it down.
1. Auto Loans & Insurance (45 sec)
* Loan vs. Owned Outright: If you finance a car, lenders require full coverage insurance, including collision and comprehensive. If you own outright, coverage is optional but recommended.
* Types of Coverage:
   * Liability: Covers damages to others.
   * Collision: Covers damages to your car.
   * Comprehensive: Covers theft, weather damage, etc.
* Additional Coverage: Consider Gap insurance (covers the difference if your car is totaled and loan balance exceeds value), roadside assistance, or special coverage for delivery driving.
* Payments: You can either pay for auto insurance as a monthly bill or in bulk for six months at a time. Get a discount for paying six months at once but need to have funds available. Costs more to pay each month, but smaller payments may be more manageable for budgets
2. Home Loans & Insurance (45 sec)
* Loan Requirements: Mortgage lenders require homeowners insurance and often PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) if your down payment is less than 20%.
* Types of Mortgages: Fixed-rate, adjustable-rate, FHA, VA, etc.
* Escrow: Many lenders include property taxes and insurance in an escrow account for easier budgeting.
* Coverage Considerations: Standard policies may not cover floods, earthquakes, or high-value personal items—assess your location’s risks before purchasing.   
3. Renters Insurance (45 sec)
* How It Works: Covers personal belongings and liability in case of theft, damage, or accidents.
* Types Available: Basic policies cover personal property, liability, and temporary living expenses if displaced.
* What to Include: Document expensive or unique items (jewelry, electronics, collectibles) and ensure they are covered under policy limits.
Conclusion (30 sec)
Whether you’re financing a car, buying a home, or renting, the right insurance protects your financial investment. Review coverage needs, compare policies, and ensure you’re fully protected before committing.
