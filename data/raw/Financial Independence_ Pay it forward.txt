Pay It Forward
Introduction (30 sec)
Financial literacy is a powerful tool, but not everyone has access to the same knowledge. By openly discussing finances and sharing what we learn, we can break down barriers, reduce stigma, and help others build a better future.
1. The Power of Generational Knowledge (45 sec)
* Studies show that children who receive financial education at home are more likely to make smart money choices as adults.
* However, only 23% of U.S. adults received financial education before turning 18.
* Wealth gaps are often knowledge gapspassing down financial wisdom can help break cycles of financial struggle.
2. Reducing Stigma Through Open Conversations (45 sec)
* Many people avoid talking about money due to shame, uncertainty, or cultural norms.
* Speaking openly about finances makes it easier to ask for help and offer support.
* Sharing budgeting tips, investment strategies, and personal experiences creates a culture where financial well-being is a shared goal.
3. Reflection Questions: How Can You Share? (45 sec)
Consider how you can pass on what you’ve learned:
* Who in your life might benefit from a conversation about finances?
   * Where are safe spaces to discuss personal experiences
   * Describe what is an appropriate response when receiving stories without ostracizing their feelings
* What financial lesson has impacted you the most, and how can you share it?
   * Are you aware of how relatable your story is to your target audience?
* Can you mentor, teach, or support someone with financial advice or resources?
Conclusion (30 sec)
Financial knowledge is a giftone that grows when shared. Whether by mentoring a friend, discussing savings strategies with family, or simply being open about challenges and successes, you have the power to help others build a stronger financial future. How will you pay it forward?
