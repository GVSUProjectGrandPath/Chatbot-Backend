Income Taxes
[0:00 - 0:15] Introduction
* The importance of understanding income taxes and how they impact your finances.
* Overview of key topics: HR forms, tax terms, deadlines, tax brackets, filing resources, and payment options.
[0:15 - 0:45] Completing Standard HR Forms (W-4 & I-9)
* [0:15 - 0:30] W-4 Form – Determines federal tax withholding from your paycheck.
   * How to fill it out correctly based on dependents and income.
   * Adjusting withholdings to avoid large tax bills or refunds.
* [0:30 - 0:45] I-9 Form – Verifies employment eligibility in the U.S.
   * Required documents: Passport, Social Security card, driver’s license, or birth certificate.
   * University or employer resources for assistance.
[0:45 - 1:15] Key Tax Terms, Brackets, and Deadlines
* [0:45 - 1:00] Tax Brackets – Understanding progressive tax rates. 
   * How income is taxed in different brackets, not a flat percentage.
* [1:00 - 1:15] Important Deadlines –
   * January 31 – Employers send W-2 forms.
   * April 15 – Federal tax filing deadline (unless extended).
   * October 15 – Extension deadline (if requested).
[1:15 - 1:45] Resources for Filing & Seeking Assistance
* [1:15 - 1:30] VITA (Volunteer Income Tax Assistance) – Free tax prep for students & low-income individuals.
* [1:30 - 1:45] Other filing resources: IRS Free File, TurboTax, H&R Block, university tax workshops.
[1:45 - 2:15] Special Tax Forms & Payment Options
* [1:45 - 2:00] 1098-T Form (Tuition Statement) – Helps students or parents claim education-related tax credits.
* [2:00 - 2:15] IRS Installment Agreements – Payment plans for those who owe taxes but can’t pay in full.
[2:15 - 3:00] Conclusion & Next Steps
* Recap key points: HR forms, tax terms, deadlines, resources, and payment plans.
* Encourage early preparation and seeking help when needed.
* Call to action: “Take control of your taxesknow your forms, use resources like VITA (The IRS Volunteer Income Tax Assistance), and file on time!”

Key Takeaways:
* W-4: Controls how much tax is withheld. Adjust it based on dependents and income.

* I-9: Verifies work eligibility, bring the right ID.

* Tax Brackets: You’re taxed in chunks, not all at once.

* Deadlines: W-2 by Jan 31, file by April 15, extension until Oct 15.

* Help is out there: Use VITA, IRS Free File, or TurboTax.

* Student Benefits: Don’t miss the 1098-T for education credits.
