Money Management – Bridging Income Gaps
1. Introduction: Understanding Income Inequality (1:00  min)
* Definition and Trends:
   * Income inequality refers to the uneven distribution of income within a population. 
   * In the U.S., income inequality has increased by about 20% from 1980 to 2016. 
* Impact on Individuals:
   * Widening income gaps can limit access to essential resources, affecting overall financial stability.


2. Strategies to Bridge Personal Income Gaps (2:00 min)
* Enhance Financial Literacy:
   * Improving financial knowledge enables informed decision-making, leading to better money management and wealth accumulation. 
   * Utilize budgeting apps and online banking tools to track expenses and savings effectively. 
* Invest in Education and Skill Development:
   * Pursuing higher education and acquiring new skills can lead to better job opportunities and increased earning potential.
* Explore Multiple Income Streams:
   * Engage inside hustles or freelance work to supplement primary income.
   * Invest in assets like stocks or real estate to generate passive income.
* Advocate for Equal Pay and Opportunities:
   * Support policies promoting pay transparency and equity in the workplace.
   * Participate in discussions and initiatives addressing systemic income disparities.


3. Leveraging Community Resources (1:00 min)
* Financial Education Programs:
   * Participate in workshops and seminars offered by community organizations to enhance financial literacy.
* Utilize Support Services:
   * Seek assistance from non-profits and community centers offering career counseling and job placement services.
* Generally each school will have somewhere to go - student affairs, dean of students, financial aid
* Example, some schools have a hardship request form
* dean of students offers more specific support - ex homelessness. Work together to figure out the specifics of how to support the student
* On campus food banks or other free resources
* student employment services to get income
   * Doesn’t have to be a dream job, just needs to help pay the bills
   * Studies show more successful if you work while in school even just 5 hours - time management, needs met




4. Takeaway: Empowering Personal Financial Growth (1:00 min)
* Continuous Learning:
   * Stay informed about financial management practices and evolving economic trends.
   * Do your own research, watch youtube videos or use google to teach yourself about different kinds of banking apps, the basic components of a loan, a mortgage, investing, etc.
* Community Engagement:
   * Collaborate with local organizations to promote financial education and address income inequality.
   * The IRS provides services like VITA where they prepare your taxes for free and can allow you to learn about it.
   * Financial literacy is also often offered at public libraries, community centers, or the city/county websites
* Proactive Planning:
   * Regularly assess personal financial goals and adjust strategies to ensure progress toward bridging income gaps.
