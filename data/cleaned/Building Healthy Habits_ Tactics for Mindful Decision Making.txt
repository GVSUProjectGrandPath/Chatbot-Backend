Tactics for Mindful Decision Making (2-3 Minute Course)
1. Introduction and Key Takeaways
* Learning Objectives:
 * Learn how mindfulness can improve decision-making.
 * Explore practical tactics for making mindful and informed decisions.
 * Understand simple trauma-informed practices to support mindfulness.
* Key Concepts:
 * Circle of Control: Focus on what you can control.
 * Benefits of Mindfulness in Decision Making: How being present can help you make better choices.
 * Practical Mindfulness Tips: Simple practices to improve self-awareness and decision-making.
2. What’s Next in the Learning Journey
* The Circle of Control Exercise:
Have you ever made a quick decision and later regretted it? Let’s talk about how mindfulness can help you make better choices in everyday life. One key exercise to help manage decisions effectively is the
* Circle of Control. This exercise involves identifying three circles (1):
 * Inner Circle (Control): Things you can directly control, such as your actions, thoughts, and reactions.
 * Examples: Submitting your FAFSA on time, applying for scholarships, creating a spending plan, reviewing your financial aid package, and setting savings goals.
 * Middle Circle (Influence): Things you can influence, like the behavior of others or certain outcomes.
 * Examples: Writing a compelling scholarship essay, asking mentors for letters of recommendation, giving feedback to your university about tuition concerns, joining student organizations to advocate for financial reform.
 * Outer Circle (Concern): Things you can’t control, like other people’s actions or uncontrollable events. The goal is to focus on what’s in your inner circle, empowering yourself to make clear and informed decisions.
 * Examples: Tuition increases, inflation, federal financial aid policies, how scholarship committees make final decisions.
 * Benefits of Mindfulness on Decision Making:
Studies show that mindfulness improves decision-making by reducing emotional reactivity, improving focus, and enhancing self-regulation. When we practice mindfulness, we are more likely to make rational decisions, avoid impulsive reactions, and understand the long-term effects of our choices. Mindfulness helps reduce stress and anxiety, enabling clearer, more thoughtful decisions.
3. Personal Reflection and Action
 * Tactics for Mindful Decision Making:
 * Clarify Your Goals and Values: Before making a decision, take time to understand your core values and long-term goals. This foundation will guide your choices and prevent impulsive decisions that don’t align with your true priorities.
 * Use the 5 Whys Technique (3): This simple technique helps you dig deeper into the root cause of a decision. By asking “Why?” five times, you get closer to understanding the underlying reason behind your decisions, helping you make more informed, thoughtful choices.
 * Practice Self-Awareness: Mindfulness encourages self-awareness, helping you recognize your emotions, triggers, and reactions. By understanding your inner state, you can make decisions that are aligned with your values and prevent knee-jerk reactions.
 * Tips for Seeking Support (2):
A few resources on campus to leverage include:
 * Wellness coaching: Wellness coaching is a free service for GVSU students. Working with your coach, you will identify strategies to support your wellness goals through a collaborative, unbiased partnership.
 * University Counseling Center: GVSU has a team of trained professionals committed to improving the mental health of the GVSU student body through prevention, education, and counseling, free of charge.
 * MoneySmart Lakers: Through presentations and individual appointments, this resource strives to deliver the best financial literacy tools to the Grand Valley community, to empower individuals and ensure their smooth transition from college to career.
4. Conclusion and Encouragement
 * Summary of Key Takeaways:
Mindful decision-making helps you make choices that align with your values and goals, while reducing stress and impulsivity. The Circle of Control, 5 Whys, and self-awareness are simple tools you can use to make more thoughtful decisions.
 * Encouragement for Next Steps:
Remember that mindfulness is a skill that improves over time. By incorporating small practices into your daily routine, you’ll gradually become more mindful and intentional in your decisions. Stay patient and compassionate with yourself as you build this habit.
 * Call to Action:
Begin with one simple mindfulness technique todayperhaps try the 5 Whys with a recent decision or use the Circle of Control to evaluate what’s within your power. Reflect on how these practices can support your decision-making in both personal and academic settings.
