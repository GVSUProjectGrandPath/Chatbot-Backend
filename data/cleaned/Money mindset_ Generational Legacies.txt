Generational Legacies: A 2-3 Minute Course Outline
I. Introduction
* Briefly define generational legacies: the passing down of knowledge, wealth, and trauma across generations.
* Ask: How has your family history shaped your financial mindset?
II. Understanding Wealth Inequality
* Key Data Points:
 * Generational wealth impacts education, homeownership, and career mobility.
 * Wealth inequality trends (St. Louis Fed, CBO reports).
 * Racial and gender disparities in wealth accumulation.
* Barriers to Wealth Building:
 * Historic and systemic issues (e.g., redlining, discriminatory hiring).
 * Economic events affecting past generations (e.g., 2008 recession, student debt crisis).
III. Reflection & Personal Impact
* What privileges and/or barriers have you inherited?
* Are you financially independent or supported by family?
* What external factors have shaped your financial situation?
IV. Action Plan & Next Steps
* Strategies for financial literacy and independence.
* Resources for overcoming barriers (e.g., scholarships, wealth-building programs).
* Encouraging intergenerational conversations about money.
V. Closing Thought
* Understanding your financial story is the first step in shaping your future.
* Ask: What’s one thing you can do today to improve your financial future?
