Money Mindset: Behavior That Prevent Progress (2-3 Minute Course)

1. Introduction: Students often struggle financially due to procrastination, emotional spending, and short-term thinking. These habits, reinforced by instant gratification, create long-term setbacks. Changing your mindset takes time, but awareness is the first step toward financial progress.
* Objective: Students will recognize limiting financial behaviors, understand systemic influences, and apply small steps to build better money habits.

2. The Goldfish Problem – Why We Struggle with Money
* The myth of overnight success vs. reality—small habits shape financial future.
* College students thrive on instant gratification, but money growth is a long game.
* Money mindsets are about behaviors, not just knowledge.

3. The Invisible Traps – Common Mindsets That Hold You Back (1:00 min)
* “I’ll figure it out later” – Avoiding financial literacy leads to costly mistakes.
 * Avoiding financial literacy can lead to costly mistakes. Financial stress has been linked to negative academic outcomes and mental health issues among college students.
* “I’m bad with money” – Fixed mindset vs. growth mindset in financial skills.
 * Students from low-income backgrounds often face systemic barriers to college access, which can affect their financial behaviors and attitudes.
* “I deserve this” (Emotional Spending) – How spending habits are shaped by feelings.
 * Financial stress can lead to unhealthy behaviors, including reduced physical activity and binge drinking.
* “If I just make more, I’ll be fine” – Why income alone won’t fix bad financial habits.
 * Financial constraints can affect students’ navigation of complex processes required to succeed in higher education, indicating that income alone won't fix financial habits.

4. The Bigger Picture – Systemic Barriers & Financial Realities
* Financial systems aren’t designed for everyone to succeed equally.
 * Low income and nontraditional students face systemic barriers to college access, including financial constraints and lack of support, which can impact their financial behaviors.
* Recognizing limitations can shift blame from self to strategy.

* Understanding credit, debt, and wealth-building—what actually matters for students.
 * Financial anxiety among college students has been linked to negative academic outcomes and mental health issues, highlighting the need to demonstrate financial concepts in their everyday life.

5. . Takeaway: Small Shifts, Big Impact (~30 sec)
* Financial progress is slow but steady—focus on habits, not hacks.
* Start with one small step: budgeting app, tracking expenses, or setting one savings goal.
* Awareness is power—change begins when you challenge limiting beliefs.
 * Building Financial Habits: Financial behavior affects the quality of life of college students, and small, positive changes can lead to significant improvements over time.
