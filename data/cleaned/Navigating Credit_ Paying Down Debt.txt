Paying Down Debt
 Introduction & Recap
* Debt is not a moral failing—it’s often due to circumstances like medical bills, job loss, or unexpected expenses.
* The key is having a plan to manage and reduce debt over time.
 Payment Plan Strategies
* Avalanche Method (Highest Interest First)
 * Pay extra on the highest interest rate debt first.
 * Saves the most money in the long run.
* Snowball Method (Smallest Balance First)
 * Pay off smallest debts first for quick wins.
 * Reduces monthly payments and boosts motivation.
 Managing Financial Hardship
* Non-Profit Debt Counseling & Repayment Plans
 * Organizations like NFCC (National Foundation for Credit Counseling) can help.
 * Collection agencies may offer negotiated repayment plans.
* Debt Consolidation
 * Combines multiple debts into one loan with a lower interest rate.
 * Can simplify payments but may extend repayment time.
 * Although this may cost more over time, it is a huge help if you are struggling to make ends meet on a monthly basis and you can generally pay them off early without penalties if you are able to make extra payments.
 Refinancing for a Lower Interest Rate
* Options to refinance loans or credit cards:
 * Balance transfer credit cards (0% APR promo offers).
 * Note, usually if you do not pay off the balance by the time the temporary 0% promo ends not only do you start accruing interest, you also have to pay back any interest you would have accrued during the 0% timeframe
 * Personal loans to consolidate high-interest debt.
 * Refinancing student loans (if it lowers rates without losing benefits).
 * Refinancing collateralized loans - if you have a car that you own in full or the loan is less than the value of the vehicle, you may be able to get an auto loan on it for the value of the vehicle and use the money for other expenses. This allows you to get a lower interest rate than an unsecured loan
 * Not a great idea if you don’t plan on having the vehicle long term because now you need to sell it for enough money to pay off the loan. Also you have to have full coverage car insurance if you have a loan, so may impact rates if you only have “personal liability and property damage (PLPD)” coverage
 * Example: I have an emergency expense for $1,000 and have own a car outright valued at $5,000. I can get an auto loan for $1,000 using the car as collateral. It is low risk to the bank because the loan is for less than the value of the car and I get a lower rate than a personal loan.
 Deferring or Pausing Student Loans
* Federal student loans may qualify for:
 * Deferment (pauses payments, may not accrue interest).
 * Forbearance (pauses payments but accrues interest).
 * Income-driven repayment plans lower monthly payments.
 Should I Keep Old Credit Accounts Open?
* Yes, in most cases:
 * Helps with credit history length (15% of FICO score).
 * Keeps credit utilization low, improving credit score.
* Close only if it has high fees or encourages overspending.
 Conclusion & Key Takeaways
* Debt repayment is about strategy, not shame.
* Choose a repayment plan that fits your situation.
* Seek assistance if needed—options exist to lower payments or rates.
* Final takeaway: "Consistency and planning are key—small steps lead to financial freedom!"

Paying Down Debt
 Debt, contrary to popular belief, is not always your fault. Oftentimes, it is due to circumstances like medical bills, job loss, or unexpected expenses. The key to managing debt is to have a plan.
 The avalanche method consists of paying extra on debts with the highest interest rate. This saves the most money in the long run as these debts get paid off. Meanwhile, the snowball method attacks the lowest payments first for small victories and to reduce monthly payments. Either plan is a valid strategy.
 For additional help, nonprofit organizations or debt consolidation are viable options to manage financial hardship. Refinancing your loan or credit card can also help consolidate your debts and help with a lower interest rate. Federal student loans can also qualify for deferments or forbearances.
 Debt repayment is about picking a plan to fit your situation. There’s no shame in seeking assistance if needed. Remember: consistency and planning are key. Small steps lead to financial freedom!
