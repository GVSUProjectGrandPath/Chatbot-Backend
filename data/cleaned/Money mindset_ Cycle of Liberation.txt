Course Outline: The Cycle of Liberation & Our Money Mindset
1. Introduction
* Define the Cycle of Liberation (Based on Bobbie Harro’s work).
* Explain how our financial beliefs are shaped through socialization.
 * “How does your past, impact your future decisions”
* Introduce the core idea: At the center of liberation is self-love.
2. Understanding the Cycle of Liberation
* Intrapersonal Level: Changing how we see ourselves in relation to money
 * Identifying limiting beliefs (e.g., “I’m bad with money,” “Wealth isn’t for people like me”).
 * Ex: Scarcity Mindset
 * Recognizing financial anxiety, fear, or stigmatization rooted in societal messages.
* Interpersonal Level: Challenging external influences
 * Examining systemic barriers (wage gaps, lack of financial education, generational wealth gaps).Recognizing obstacles that seem to be hard to overcome because it has been imposed by society. Being able to break the chain of the past.
 * Building financial literacy and engaging in supportive communities.(The building process of new habits is not always easy, but it never has to be fast. Small steps also leads to success)
* Systemic Level: Making structural changes
 * Advocating for financial equity and accessible financial education.
 * Understanding privilege and dismantling financial oppression.
3. Breaking the Cycle & Creating Financial Liberation
* Awareness: Recognizing how systemic factors shape financial behaviors.
* Action: Making conscious financial choices to break unhealthy cycles.(Taking first small steps towards building a stable relationship with money)
* Transformation: Developing a mindset of abundance and empowerment.
* Key question: What do you gain by embracing financial liberation?
 * More confidence, financial security, reduced stress, and increased freedom.
4. Conclusion
* Reinforce the core message: Financial liberation starts with self-love and conscious action.
* Encourage participants to challenge and transform their money mindset.
5. Reference Materials

Hey, so this course is all about how we think about money and how we can change that thinking to feel more free and confident with it.
We start with this idea called the Cycle of Liberation, based on Bobbie Harro’s work. Basically, a lot of our money habits and beliefs—like thinking we’re bad with money or that wealth isn’t for people like us—come from how we were raised and what society teaches us.
First, we look at how we see ourselves: do we have a scarcity mindset or fear around money? Then we move to how outside forces like income gaps and lack of access to financial education shape our money situation. Finally, we talk about how to push for bigger changes—like making financial education more accessible.
The whole point is to break out of unhealthy money cycles by being aware, taking small steps, and building a new, empowered mindset. It all starts with self-love and deciding you deserve financial freedom.
