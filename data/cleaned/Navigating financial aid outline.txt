Navigating financial aid

Here’s a 2-3 minute video outline based on your slideshow “MSL 2024 Financial Aid Basics and Paying the Bill”:

Video Title:
Financial Aid Basics and Paying Your College Bill: What You Need to Know

Introduction
* Greet and briefly introduce the topic:
"Let’s quickly cover the essentials of financial aid and how you can manage your college bill."

Part 1: Understanding Financial Aid
 * FAFSA Basics:

 * Complete the FAFSA every year starting Dec 1st.

 * Use tax info from two years prior (example: file in 2024 for 2025-26 with 2023 taxes).

 * Student Aid Index (SAI) & Cost of Attendance (COA):

 * SAI = eligibility number based on FAFSA.

 * COA = tuition + living expenses; it's a budget estimate, not your actual bill.

Part 2: Types of Financial Aid
 * Grants = free money (need-based).

 * Scholarships = free money (merit-based).

 * Loans = borrowed money to repay (Federal Direct Loans, Parent PLUS).

 * Work-Study = campus jobs to earn money while studying.

Part 3: Special Programs
 * Check out state specific programs
 * Example Michigan Achievement Scholarship: Up to $5,500/year for eligible students.

 * Tuition Incentive Program (TIP): Support for Medicaid-eligible students.

 * Check out college specific programs
 * Example: GVSU myScholarships: Over 500 scholarships – apply between November and March.

Part 4: Paying the Bill
 * Know the payment schedule at your college
 * GVSU Bills sent November 13 for Winter Semester.
 * Payment deadline = December 13.
 * Options: Pay in full, or Enroll in the 4-payment deferment plan ($40 fee per semester).

Part 5:Tips
 * Manage Loans Wisely:

 * Pay interest early.

 * Make a budget.

 * Know your servicer.

 * Pay more than the minimum if possible.

Conclusion
 * Encourage action:
"Stay ahead by applying early, managing your money smartly, and asking for help if you need it."
