Brooklyn Rent Strikes
 Every month, landlords come to collect rent. This rent money is used to maintain the building, to repair amenities, and to keep the units safe. As expected with inflation, most rent increases happen annually during lease renewal to keep pace with rising costs.
 Back in April 2023, a company called Raj Associates expected to collect rent from 45 tenants in a Brooklyn apartment building. To their surprise, however, they only found payments from 12 tenants. The other tenants had complaints about a faulty front door that wasn’t being addressed, broken washers and dryers that weren’t being fixed, and about a rent hike that, in some cases, was around $500. In response, 33 people had banded together and agreed to stop paying rent until their complaints were heard. After a six month struggle, the rent hikes were drastically lowered, and management agreed to put money into repairs. In return, these tenants paid all the rent that was owed.
 This is called collective bargaining. One or two or even three people refusing to pay rent is easy to ignore, but when large groups of people are organized in their protest, they must be taken seriously. By banding together, these tenants were able to make their complaints heard and remind the larger company that there were consequences to disregarding tenants’ rights.
