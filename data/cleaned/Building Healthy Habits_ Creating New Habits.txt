Creating New Financial Habits (2-3 Minute Course)
1. Introduction and Key Takeaways
* Learning Objectives:
 * Understand the importance of setting aside time for financial management.
 * Learn effective activities to work on your finances during this dedicated time.
 * Explore tips and resources for students with neurodiversity or challenges forming new habits.
* Key Concepts:
 * Time for Finances: Setting aside intentional time each week for finances is crucial for building long-term financial habits.
 * Living Below Your Means: Focus on spending less than you earn, a core principle of financial stability.
 * Realistic Goal Setting: Learn how to set achievable financial goals that fit your lifestyle.
2. What’s Next in the Learning Journey (5)
* Setting Time Aside for Finances:
To motivate students who feel like managing finances is pointless, it's important to reframe the process. Instead of focusing on the amount, emphasize the habit itself and the progress over time. Suggest setting aside intentional time each weeklike an hour on Sunday or quick 15-minute check-insto review finances, track small wins, and adjust goals. The key is consistency, and even small steps can build momentum. Celebrate progress, no matter how small, to keep motivation high.
 * Tip: Make it a non-negotiable part of your routine, just like a class or an important meeting.
 * The 'Zeigarnik Effect': Starting a task, even briefly, can create a mental reminder to return to it. Our brains tend to remember unfinished tasks.
 * Incentives: Focus on the benefits of managing your finances: peace of mind, less stress about money, and more control over your future. Use this as motivation to prioritize finances over distractions like doom-scrolling on social media.
 * Types of Activities During Finance Time:
 * Budgeting: Start by tracking your income and expenses. There are many apps, such as Mint (1) or YNAB (You Need A Budget) (2), that can help you categorize and monitor your spending (6).
 * Saving: Set up automatic transfers to a savings account. Even small amounts, like $5 or $10 a week, can add up over time.
 * Debt Management: If you have student loans or credit card debt, use this time to plan your payments and reduce interest.
 * Financial Goals: Write down short-term goals (like saving for a trip) and long-term goals (like building an emergency fund or saving for retirement).
 * Tip: Keep it simple. It’s not about being perfect; it’s about taking small, consistent steps.
3. Personal Reflection and Action
 * Tips for Neurodiversity or Struggles with Habit Formation (3, 4):
 * Create a Predictable Routine: Set specific, consistent times for finance tasks using reminders or visual cues. Share those goals with friends and family to help you stay consistent and accountable.
 * Break Tasks into Manageable Steps: Start small, like reviewing spending or setting a basic budget, to avoid overwhelm.
 * Use Technology: Leverage apps and reminders to stay organized, track progress, and gamify saving.
 * Seek Accountability and Support: Share your goals with someone for motivation and check-ins.
4. Conclusion and Encouragement
 * Summary of Key Takeaways:
Creating healthy financial habits starts with setting aside intentional time for finances, choosing small activities like budgeting, saving, or tracking spending. The consistency of these small efforts is what will lead to lasting change.
 * Encouragement for Next Steps:
Remember, the goal is to build sustainable habits, not perfection. Set a small goal today, like reviewing your spending for the last month or setting up an automatic savings transfer.
 * Call to Action:
Stay patient with yourself as you navigate the world of personal finance. With consistent effort, even the smallest steps will lead to big changes over time. Make that time for your finances nowit’s an investment in your future!
