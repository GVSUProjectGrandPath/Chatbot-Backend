Credit Cards as a Financial Tool
 Introduction
* Credit cards can be a powerful financial tool when used wisely.
* Overview of key topics: building credit, avoiding debt, selecting the right card, and maximizing rewards.
 Using Credit Cards to Build Long-Term Payment History
* A credit card helps establish payment history, which is 35% of your FICO score. Because it is an open term loan, this allows you to long term payment history
* Key habits for building credit:
 * Always pay on time to avoid late fees and damage to your score.
 * Keep credit utilization low (below 30% of your limit).
 Tips for Using Credit Cards Without Taking on Debt
* Treat your credit card like a debit cardonly charge what you can pay off in full.
* Pay off the full statement balance each month to avoid paying interest
 * A cash advance is when you transfer money from the credit card into your account, to a third party like a payment app (venmo, cash app), any transfer processed like an ACH (electronic check). Cash advance interest begins accruing immediately, so charged interest even if you pay the full balance each month
* Set up automatic payments to avoid missing due dates.
 * If carrying a balance, prioritize paying more than the minimum to reduce interest costs.
 * If you don’t need to use credit much or are concerned about overspending, set up one recurring monthly bill to be paid with your credit card automatically (a streaming service, your phone bill, a utility, etc.) and an automatic payment for the credit card each month. That will prevent the card from going dormant and allows you to make on time monthly payments without taking on debt.
 What to Look for When Selecting a Credit Card
* Consider:
 * Interest rate (APR): Lower is better if you might carry a balance.
 * * Annual fees: Avoid unless the rewards outweigh the cost.
 * Credit limit: A higher limit can help with credit utilization.
* Student or secured credit cards are great for beginners.
 * Some institutions offer preferred rates for students knowing that they will have lower scores to start but have higher earning potential in the future because they are pursuing education
 * Secured credit cards allow you to put your own money on hold equal to the credit limit of the card. This allows to the institution to offer lower rates like a collateralized loan while the borrower can begin building credit history
 Credit Card Rewards & Benefits
* Types of rewards:
 * Cash back: Earn a percentage on everyday purchases.
 * Points & miles: Great for travel perks.
* Use rewards responsiblydon’t overspend just to earn points.
 * Reward cards are best used if you are able to pay them off every month because you can get the rewards and not pay interest. If you plan to carry a balance, stick to a normal card so you pay less money in interest. Rewards cards tend to have higher rates and annual fees to offset the cost to the lender
 Conclusion & Key Takeaways
* Recap: Credit cards build credit, require responsible use, and can offer valuable rewards.
* Final takeaway: "Use credit cards as a toolnot a trap. Pay on time, spend wisely, and take advantage of the benefits!”
