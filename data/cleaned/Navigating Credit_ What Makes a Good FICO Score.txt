What Makes a Good FICO Score
 Introduction
* Importance of understanding your FICO score and how it impacts financial opportunities.
* Overview of key topics: how scores are calculated, key factors affecting your credit, what’s on your report, and what a good score looks like for a college student.
 How FICO Scores Are Calculated
* Each credit bureau (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) uses slightly different formulas.
 * Exact formulas are trade secrets, but general factors are known.
* FICO scores range from 300 to 850, with higher scores indicating lower credit risk.
 Most Important Factors for Building Credit
* Key factors that impact your score:
 * Payment history (35%) – Pay bills on time!
 * Credit utilization (30%) – Keep balances low compared to your credit limit.
 * Tip: Keep at least 50% of card limit available
 * Length of credit history (15%) – The longer, the better.
 * Credit mix (10%) – Variety of accounts (credit cards, student loans, etc.).
 * New credit inquiries (10%) – Too many hard inquiries can lower your score.
 * Tip: For auto and home loans, they expect you to rate shop, so credit pulls for those loans within a 14 day window count as one pull
* The most important thing for college students is on-time payments and low credit utilization.
 What’s on Your Credit Report (Besides Loans)?
* Other reported items:
 * Credit cards, student loans, rental history (sometimes), and even utility bills.
* Missed payments, collections, and hard inquiries stay on your report for years.
 What Is a Good FICO Score for a College Student?
* Unlike adults with mortgages and car loans, most students have limited credit history.
* A good college student FICO score is typically 650-720—high enough to qualify for credit cards and low-interest loans.
 Conclusion & Next Steps
* Recap: Scores vary by bureau, key factors like payment history and utilization, and what’s on your report.
* Encourage checking credit reports regularly at AnnualCreditReport.com and using free monitoring apps.
* Final takeaway: "A good FICO score starts with small, smart habits—pay on time, use credit wisely, and build for the future!”
Your FICO score matters more than you think it can affect your ability to rent an apartment, get a car, or land low-interest loans.
FICO scores range from 300 to 850, and while each credit bureau calculates it slightly differently, the key factors are the same. The most important? Paying bills on time and keeping your credit card balances low. That alone makes up over 60% of your score.
Other things that matter: how long you’ve had credit, the types of accounts you have, and how often your credit is pulled.
Your report can also include student loans, credit cards, and sometimes even utilities or rent.
For college students, a score between 650 and 720 is solid—it shows you’re responsible, even with limited credit history.
Start by checking your credit with a free monitoring app or through your bank. Smart habits now lead to stronger credit and better financial options down the road.
