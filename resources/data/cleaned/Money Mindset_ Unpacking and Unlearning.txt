Unpacking and Unlearning Behaviors (2-3 Minute Course)
1. Introduction
* Topic Introduction: Briefly introduce the concept of "unlearning" and why it’s important for personal growth. Have you ever caught yourself repeating a habit you know isn’t helping you, but you do it anyway?
* Objective: Help students understand how unlearning unhelpful behaviors and adopting healthier ones can lead to improved well-being and success.
2. Steps to Unlearn Behaviors That Do Not Serve Us
* Recognizing Unhelpful Behaviors:
 * Identify behaviors or thought patterns that hold you back (e.g., procrastination, perfectionism, negative self-talk) (2).
 * Example: A student might recognize that they have a tendency to procrastinate when overwhelmed, which impacts their academic performance.
* Understanding the Root Causes:
 * Investigate why these behaviors existare they learned from childhood, societal expectations, or past experiences?
 * Example: Procrastination could stem from a fear of failure or feeling incapable.
* Letting Go of Old Patterns:
 * Be mindful of when these behaviors arise and consciously choose not to act on them.
 * Tip: Use journaling or self-reflection techniques to track moments when unhelpful behaviors emerge.
3. Steps to Learn More Helpful Behaviors
* Adopt New, Healthy Habits:
 * Identify behaviors that would benefit your life (e.g., setting boundaries, practicing self-compassion, time management).
 * Example: Instead of procrastinating, the student can start breaking tasks into smaller, more manageable steps to reduce anxiety (3).
* Practice and Repetition:
 * Reinforce new behaviors through consistent practice, even when it feels uncomfortable.
 * Tip: Start small by incorporating new behaviors into daily routines (e.g., setting a timer for focused study sessions) (4).
* Seek Support:
 * Surround yourself with people who support your growth and model positive behaviors.
 * Example: Joining a study group can encourage better time management and accountability.
4. Personal Reflection
* Guided Reflection:
 * Encourage students to think about their own behaviors and how they impact their daily life, relationships, and well-being.
 * Questions for Reflection:
 * What are some behaviors I’ve noticed that don’t serve me (e.g., procrastination, self-doubt, avoiding confrontation)?
 * How do these behaviors show up in my life (e.g., affecting school, relationships, personal goals)?
 * What healthier behaviors would I like to adopt, and why?
 * What small steps can I take today to start unlearning and replacing these behaviors?
* Encourage Journaling: Give students a moment to write down their answers and reflect on their personal growth journey.
5. Conclusion and Takeaways
* Summarize Key Points:
 * Unlearning is a process of self-awareness and intentional change. By identifying old habits, understanding their roots, and replacing them with healthier alternatives, we can grow and evolve (1).
* Call to Action:
 * Encourage students to practice self-compassion during their journey of unlearning and to celebrate small wins along the way.
 * Invite students to reflect regularly on their behaviors and make adjustments as needed.
