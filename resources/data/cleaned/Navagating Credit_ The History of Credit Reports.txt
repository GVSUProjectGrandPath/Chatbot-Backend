The History of Credit Reports
 Introduction
* Briefly introduce the importance of credit reports and their impact on financial life.
* Overview of key topics: history of the credit system, biases in the system, and how credit reports are used beyond loans.
 Overview of the History of the Credit System
* Early credit systems:
 * 19th-century local lenders relied on personal reputation and word-of-mouth.
 * Rise of formal credit reporting agencies in the 20th century.
* Development of modern credit bureaus:
 * Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion dominate the industry.
 * Credit scores became standardized with the FICO system (1989).
 Bias in the Credit System
* Credit data is collected and sold by private companies.
 * Consumers have no choice but to participate.
 * Errors in reports can negatively impact individuals without their knowledge.
* Historical and systemic biases:
 * Early credit systems excluded women and minorities.
 * Credit scoring models may still reflect financial inequality.
 Understanding How Your Credit Report Is Used
* More than just loans:
 * Affects job applications, insurance rates, housing approvals, and even utility bills.
 * For payments that aren’t related to a loan (utilities, rent, etc.), they don’t usually show up on your credit if you pay them on-time, but they will show up if they send you to collections. So poor payment history can hurt your credit score but good payment history doesn’t help it.
* Importance of monitoring and maintaining good credit.
 * Checking for errors and disputing inaccuracies.
 Conclusion & Next Steps
* Recap: Credit history evolution, biases, and how reports affect more than just borrowing.
* Encourage checking credit reports regularly through AnnualCreditReport.com.
* Final takeaway: "Understanding your credit is the first step to controlling your financial future."
