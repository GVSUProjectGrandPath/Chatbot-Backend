Student Loans Deep Dive (2-3 Minute Course Outline)
1. Introduction
* Briefly introduce the importance of understanding student loans.
* Highlight key takeaways: loan types, repayment strategies, and avoiding scams.
2. Types of Student Loans
* Federal vs. Private Loans:
 * Federal loans typically offer lower interest rates and more flexible repayment options
 * (MUST file the FAFSA form to use federal loans).
 * Private loans may have higher interest rates and fewer protections.
* Parent PLUS Loans vs. Student Loans:
 * Parents take financial responsibility for Parent PLUS Loans.
 * Student loans remain in the borrower's name.
* Subsidized vs. Unsubsidized Loans:
 * Subsidized: Interest does not accrue while in school.
 * Unsubsidized: Interest accrues immediately.
3. Planning for Repayment
* When repayment begins:
 * Federal loans: Typically 6-month grace period after graduation.
 * Private loans: Varies by lender.
* Creating a repayment strategy:
 * Include loan payments in your budget early.
 * Consider making interest payments while in school.
4. Student Loan Refunds – What to Do?
* Options for using loan refunds:
 * Apply it to the loan principal to reduce debt.
 * Use for necessary living expenses but avoid unnecessary spending.
5. Navigating Hardships
* Deferment vs. Forbearance:
 * Deferment: Temporarily stops payments, interest may not accrue (for subsidized loans).
 * Forbearance: Temporarily reduces or pauses payments, but interest continues accruing.
* Federal Relief Programs & Loan Forgiveness:
 * Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) & Income-Driven Repayment Plans.
 * How to qualify and avoid scams.
* Avoiding Loan Scams:
 * Red flags: Upfront fees, aggressive sales tactics, and promises of “immediate” loan forgiveness.
* Campus & Community Resources:
 * Financial aid office, nonprofit student loan counseling services.
6. Conclusion & Key Takeaways
* Recap key points: loan types, repayment planning, responsible use of refunds, and avoiding scams.
* Encourage proactive financial planning and seeking guidance from trusted sources.
