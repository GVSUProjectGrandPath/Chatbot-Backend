Module: Financial Independence
Lesson: Use Your Power for Good
────────────────────────────────────────

Outline for "Use Your Power for Good" Course
1. Introduction
* Revisit the initial mindset discussion: The belief that everyone deserves access to the necessities of life.
* Emphasize the importance of balancing self-investment and prioritizing well-being while making ethical choices.
* Reminder: There is no perfect way to navigate an unjust system, but your actions can create meaningful change.
2. Financial Independence and Health
* Financial independence is an active processinvest in yourself, review spending plans, monitor credit, and continually evaluate investments.
* Healthy financial habits should be a lifetime practice, not a temporary fix.
* Key takeaway: Stability starts with maintaining the basics.
3. Wealth as Power
* Wealth is a form of power. Discuss how individuals can leverage their resources for good.
* Ways to use your power:
 * Philanthropy: Contribute to causes that matter to you or invest in your local community. Highlight tax benefits of charitable giving.
 * Civic Engagement & Activism: Donate to causes or political campaigns. Mention the cost of running for office and how grassroots movements can influence change.
 * Leading Change in the Workplace: Advocate for equity, inclusivity, and positive workplace changes that benefit all employees.
4. Real-World Examples of Impact
* Gamestop Short Squeeze: Explain how retail investors rallied together to challenge Wall Street power and drive change.
* Kellogg Boycott: Discuss the impact of collective consumer action on corporate decisions.
* Key takeaway: Individuals, when united, can challenge systems and initiate change.
5. Encouraging Conversations About Finances
* Encourage learners to have conversations about finances with loved ones. Share what has worked well for you and listen to others.
* Break the stigma around money management: Open communication makes it easier to ask for help, offer support, and exchange knowledge.
6. Conclusion
* Reinforce the importance of using your powerwhether through finances, activism, or leadershipto uplift others and create lasting change.
