Setting Financial Goals
Outline for 2-3 Minute Learning Module
Title: Setting Financial Goals: A Roadmap to Financial Success
1. Introduction (0:00 - 0:30)
* Why financial goal setting matters: It provides direction, reduces stress, and builds financial security.
* Examples of when goal setting is helpful:
 * Emergency savings for unexpected expenses.
 * Saving for a large purchase (e.g., a car, laptop, or study abroad).
 * Paying down debt to improve financial freedom.
2. Using SMART Goals for Time-Sensitive Planning (0:30 - 1:30)
* Specific: Define the exact goal (e.g., "Save $500 for emergencies").
* Measurable: Track progress ($50/month for 10 months).
* Achievable: Make it realistic based on income and expenses.
* Relevant: Ensure it aligns with personal priorities.
* Time-bound: Set a clear deadline for completion.
3. Turning Good Habits into Financial Goals (1:30 - 2:30)
* Instead of only focusing on outcomes, make habits the goal:
 * Example: "Save 10% of every paycheck" instead of just "Save $1,000."
* Prioritize short-term goals (e.g., building a $100 emergency fund before tackling debt).
* Track progress often using budgeting apps, spreadsheets, or notebooks.
* Celebrate small wins to stay motivated.
4. Conclusion & Call to Action (2:30 - 3:00)
* Financial literacy success is built on consistent habits and realistic goals.
* Encourage starting with one small goal today.
* Share resources like financial goal-setting worksheets or apps (e.g., Mint, YNAB).
* Final takeaway: Small, intentional steps today lead to long-term financial success.
