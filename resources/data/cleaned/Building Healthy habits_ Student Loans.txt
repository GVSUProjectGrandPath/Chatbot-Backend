Outline for a 2-3 Minute Course on Student Loans
Introduction
* Student loans are often a student’s first major financial decision.
* Treat them like an investmentensure your potential earnings outweigh the cost.
* Good financial habits, like reviewing your finances weekly or bi-weekly, can help manage student debt wisely.
1. Understanding Student Loans as an Investment
* Loans can be a tool to access education but should be taken with a plan in mind.
* Consider future earning potential in your field versus total loan cost.
* Decision-making techniques: Cost-benefit analysis, goal setting, and budgeting.
2. Overview of Student Loan Types
* Federal Loans (FAFSA required)
 * Typically lower interest rates
 * Income-driven repayment options
 * Some loan forgiveness programs are available, but are decreasing significantly as time goes on
* Private Loans
 * Higher interest rates
 * Fewer repayment protections
 * Should only be considered after maximizing federal options
* Subsidized vs unsubsidized loans
 * Subsidized
 * Need-based
 * Government pays interest while in school, etc.
 * Undergraduate students
 * Lower loan limits
 * Unsubsidized
 * Not need-based
 * Borrower responsible for all interest from day one
 * Undergraduate and graduate students
 * Higher loan limits
3. Know Your Resources
* Campus Support – Financial aid office, student advisors, and workshops.
* Online Tools – Federal student aid website, loan calculators, financial literacy resources.
* FAFSA & Financial Aid Process –
 * Complete FAFSA early (required for federal loans and some grants).
 * Understand tuition refunds (e.g., deciding whether to send them home or keep them).
 * Prioritize federal loans before considering private ones.
 * Recognize that financial aid availability varies based on individual and societal factors.
Conclusion
* Always review financial decisions and consider the long-term impact.
* Take advantage of available resources to make informed choices.
* Smart borrowing today leads to a manageable financial future after graduation.
