Student Loans Deep Dive (2-3 Minute Course Outline)
1. Introduction (10-15 seconds)
* Briefly introduce the importance of understanding student loans.
* Highlight key takeaways: loan types, repayment strategies, and avoiding scams.
2. Types of Student Loans (30-45 seconds)
* Federal vs. Private Loans:
   * Federal loans typically offer lower interest rates and more flexible repayment options 
      * (MUST file the FAFSA form to use federal loans).
   * Private loans may have higher interest rates and fewer protections.
* Parent PLUS Loans vs. Student Loans:
   * Parents take financial responsibility for Parent PLUS Loans[a].
   * Student loans remain in the borrower's name.
* Subsidized vs. Unsubsidized Loans:
   * Subsidized: Interest does not accrue while in school.
   * Unsubsidized: Interest accrues immediately.
3. Planning for Repayment (30-45 seconds)
* When repayment begins:
   * Federal loans: Typically 6-month grace period after graduation.
   * Private loans: Varies by lender.
* Creating a repayment strategy:
   * Include loan payments in your budget early.
   * Consider making interest payments while in school.
4. Student Loan Refunds – What to Do? (15-30 seconds)
* Options for using loan refunds[b]:
   * Apply it to the loan principal to reduce debt.
   * Use for necessary living expenses but avoid unnecessary spending.
5. Navigating Hardships (30-45 seconds)
* Deferment vs. Forbearance:
   * Deferment: Temporarily stops payments, interest may not accrue (for subsidized loans).
   * Forbearance: Temporarily reduces or pauses payments, but interest continues accruing.
* Federal Relief Programs & Loan Forgiveness:
   * Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) & Income-Driven Repayment Plans.
   * How to qualify and avoid scams.[c]
* Avoiding Loan Scams:
   * Red flags: Upfront fees, aggressive sales tactics, and promises of “immediate” loan forgiveness.
* Campus & Community Resources:
   * Financial aid office, nonprofit student loan counseling services.
6. Conclusion & Key Takeaways (10-15 seconds)
* Recap key points: loan types, repayment planning, responsible use of refunds, and avoiding scams.
* Encourage proactive financial planning and seeking guidance from trusted sources.

SCRIPT
As students, taking student loans gives satisfaction and the opportunity to be able to go through college, but caution and a lot of considerations have to be taken into other so as not to lead to long-term debt.
Firstly, you need to know the basics;
* Federal loans: These are offered by the government. You will need to fill out the FAFSA to qualify, but they usually come with lower interest rates and more flexible repayment options.
* Private loans: These come from banks or lenders. They may offer fewer protections and higher interest rates, so it’s usually best to use them only after exploring all federal options.
* Parent PLUS Loan: If your parents are helping, they might be offered, but keep in mind, they are on the hook for paying it back.
* Subsidized loans: These loans don’t accrue interest while you’re in school.
* Unsubsidized loans: These start adding interest right away.


You have to make a plan early, start budgeting early, and if you can, pay extra toward the loan with the highest interest rate, which saves you money long term and ultimately,stay informed. That’s how you keep student loans from becoming lifelong baggage.


Key Takeaways
* Federal Loans = Lower interest, more protections. File the FAFSA to qualify.

* Private Loans = Fewer protections, higher interest, only after federal options.

* Parent PLUS Loans = Borrowed by a parent, not the student.

* Subsidized vs. Unsubsidized: Subsidized = no interest in school. Unsubsidized = interest starts right away.
