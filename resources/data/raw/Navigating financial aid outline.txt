Script
Hey there! Let’s quickly cover the essentials of financial aid and how to manage your college bill. First, fill out your FAFSA every year starting December 1st. You’ll need tax information from two years prior. For example, if you’re filing for the 2025-2026 academic year, use your 2023 tax return. Once submitted, you’ll receive your Student Aid Index (SAI), which determines your eligibility for aid, and your Cost of Attendance (COA), which includes tuition, living expenses, and books.
There are different types of financial aid available. Grants are free money based on need, while scholarships are merit-based and also free money. Loans are borrowed money you need to repay, such as Federal Direct Loans and Parent PLUS Loans. Work-Study offers part-time jobs on campus to help cover costs. Additionally, states like Michigan offer programs such as the Michigan Achievement Scholarship, which provides up to $5,500 per year for eligible students, and the Tuition Incentive Program (TIP) for Medicaid-eligible students.
Once your bill is issued, be aware of your college’s payment schedule. For example, GVSU sends out bills for the Winter Semester on November 13th, with a payment deadline of December 13th. You can either pay in full or use the 4-payment deferment plan for a $40 fee per semester.
Finally, manage your loans wisely by paying interest early, creating a budget, and knowing your loan servicer. It’s always a good idea to pay more than the minimum when possible.
Stay ahead by applying early, managing your finances smartly, and asking for help if you need it!
