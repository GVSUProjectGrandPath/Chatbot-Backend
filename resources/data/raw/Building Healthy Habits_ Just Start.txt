Building Healthy Habits: Just Start (2-3 Minute Course)
1. Introduction & Key Takeaways (30-40 seconds)
Core Learning Objectives:
* Understand how perfectionism can stall progress and how to leverage it positively.
* Learn the power of small, consistent actions in habit formation.
* Focus on the process, not perfection, to build lasting habits.
2. The Impact of Perfectionism on Habit Formation (40-50 seconds)
How Perfectionism Can Stall Progress (1):
* Overwhelm: When you aim for perfection, it can feel like a huge, daunting task, leading to avoidance.
* Self-doubt: You may fear failure and get discouraged, thinking “If I can’t do it perfectly, why bother?”
* All-or-nothing thinking: If we can’t do something perfectly, we might abandon it altogether.
Leveraging Perfectionism Positively:
* Use your perfectionist traits for consistency instead of flawlessness. The desire for excellence can drive you to keep improving, just not right away.
* Shift your focus from being perfect to being consistent.[a][b] Small improvements over time will lead to better results than aiming for perfection every time. Setting up a goal tracker is a good way to go, start by setting small goals that will inspire and push individuals towards great success (3).
3. Case Study Example: Small Changes Grow Over Time (40-50 seconds)
Let’s consider a real-life example: Alex, a college student, wants to develop a healthy financial habit. Initially, he thinks he needs to save a large sum every month, but when that feels overwhelming, he starts small.
Alex’s Approach:
* He starts saving just $2 a week, which feels manageable.
* Over time, he becomes more comfortable with saving and increases the amount as his confidence and finances grow. In a few months, he’s saving $100 a month, and it’s no longer a stressful habitjust a consistent one.
Key Takeaway: Starting small, even with something as tiny as $2, leads to bigger progress over time. Small actions consistently add up to big results.
4. Building the Habit: Focus on Consistency, Not Perfection (40-50 seconds) (2)
To successfully build habits, the focus should always be on consistent action, not perfection. Here’s how to start and stay on track:
* Start with tiny amounts. It might feel insignificant, but even small actions like saving $2 or exercising for 5 minutes matter in the long run.
* Focus on the habit, not the amount. Whether you save $2 or $200, the goal is to form the habit, not obsess over the scale of the action.
* Be patient with yourself. Progress takes time, and it’s okay to have setbacks. Be kind to yourself during the process.
The key to habit formation is showing up consistently, not doing everything perfectly.
5. Conclusion & Encouragement (30-40 seconds)
To summarize:
* Perfectionism can either stall or motivate you depending on how you use it. The goal is to focus on consistency, not on perfection.
* Starting small is the key. Whether it’s $2 or a 5-minute workout, tiny actions add up over time.
* Be patient and gentle with yourself as you build new habits. Change takes time, but persistence pays off.
Call to Action: Stay focused on your journey, no matter how small the steps. By showing up every day, you’ll make lasting changes. Keep building, and remember, the goal is progress, not perfection.

Extra materials to include:
Journal entries:
A simple, motivational tracker students can use daily or weekly:
* Format: Grid with dates across the top and goals listed down the side
* Includes a space for:
   * “Tiny habit” (e.g., save $2, drink water, 5-min stretch)
   * Mood check-in
   * Encouraging quote or reminder (“Progress, not perfection”)
   * Offer a “blank” version for customization + a sample with pre-filled habits
Setting goals section:
* “One habit I want to build is…”
* “What’s the tiniest version of that habit I can do?”
* “What gets in my way?”
* “What would consistency look like in 7 days?”
*Includes checkboxes and space for journaling brief reflections at the end of the week.
