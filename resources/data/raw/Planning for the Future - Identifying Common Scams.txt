Planning for the Future – Identifying Common Scams
1. Introduction: The Importance of Scam Awareness (1:00 min)
* Vulnerability of College Students:
   * Students often face financial pressures, making them prime targets for specific scams.
* Prevalence of Scams:
   * Scammers continually devise new methods to exploit individuals, making awareness crucial.
2. Common Scams Targeting College Students (2:00 min)
* Scholarship and Financial Aid Scams:
   * Fraudulent entities offer fake scholarships or financial aid services, charging fees without providing assistance. 
      * Always check the email address for suspicious senders.
* Employment Scams:
   * Scammers pose as employers offering high-paying jobs or internships, requesting personal information or upfront fees. 
* Housing and Rental Scams:
   * Fake listings for apartments or houses require deposits or personal information, leading to financial loss. 
* Phishing Emails:
   * Emails that appear legitimate aim to steal personal information or install malware. 
* Student Loan Forgiveness Scams:
   * Offers to reduce or eliminate student loan debt in exchange for fees, without delivering services. 

3. Red Flags to Identify Potential Scams (1:00 min)
   * Unsolicited Requests:
   * Unexpected communications asking for personal or financial information. 
   * Pressure Tactics:
   * Urgency to act immediately or face negative consequences. 
   * Upfront Payments:
   * Requests for fees before receiving services or products. 
   * Too Good to Be True Offers:
   * Deals or opportunities that seem unusually favorable. 
4. Steps to Protect Yourself from Scams (1:00 min)
   * Verify Legitimacy:
   * Research and confirm the authenticity of offers or communications. 
   * Reach out to colleagues for cross referencing emails. 
   * Use Official Channels:
   * Access services or information through official websites or contact points. 
   * Protect Personal Information:
   * Avoid sharing sensitive data with unverified entities. 
   * Consult Trusted Advisors:
   * Seek guidance from other students, university officials, or financial advisors when in doubt.
5. Takeaway: Staying Vigilant Against Scams (30 sec)
   * Continuous Education:
   * Regularly update yourself on emerging scam tactics. 
   * Report Suspected Scams:
   * Notify relevant authorities or consumer protection agencies about potential scams. 
   * Trust Your Instincts:
   * If something feels off, exercise caution and verify before proceeding. 
By staying informed and cautious, individuals can safeguard their financial futures against common scams to protect yourself and your personal finances. .


Identifying Common Scams
        If there’s one thing that credit card companies and scammers have in common, it’s that they both know how vulnerable college students are. Students facing financial pressure are prime targets for scams. Scammers are continually devising new methods of exploitation.
        Some common scams include fake Financial Aid emails that try to trick students into paying an application fee, and fraudulent employment emails that seek to steal personal information.
        Always keep an eye out for red flags, like unsolicited requests for personal or financial information. Some scams will also try to pressure you into acting quickly so you don’t have time to reconsider or offer deals that are too good to be true.
        To protect yourself, avoid sharing sensitive data with unverified recipients, regularly educate yourself on emerging scams, and trust your instincts if something feels off.
        By staying informed and cautious, students can safeguard their financial futures from common scams.
